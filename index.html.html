SAT Exam Overview:

  • Purpose:
    • Standardized test for college admissions in the United States
  • Sections:
    • Mathematics
    • Evidence-Based Reading and Writing (EBRW)
    • Optional Essay
  • Format:
    • Multiple-choice questions
    • Essay writing (if opted for)
  • Duration:
    • About 3 hours without the essay, 3 hours and 50 minutes with the essay
  • Scoring:
    • Math and EBRW sections scored on a scale of 200-800 each
    • Optional essay scored separately on a scale of 2-8 by two graders üìù

Subjects :-

  • Mathematics:
    • Algebra and Functions
    • Geometry and Measurement
    • Data Analysis, Statistics, and Probability
  • Evidence-Based Reading and Writing (EBRW):
    • Reading Comprehension
    • Writing and Language Skills
  • Optional Essay Writing:
    • Analytical Essay Writing
  • Overall Skills Tested:
    • Critical thinking
    • Problem-solving
    • Analytical reasoning

    Marking System:-

    Marking System in SAT with Each Subject:

    • Mathematics:
      • Marks: 200-800
    • Evidence-Based Reading and Writing (EBRW):
      • Marks: 200-800
    • Optional Essay:
      • Marks: Scored separately on a scale of 2-8 by two different graders, totaling a maximum of 24 points.
    • Total SAT Score:
      • Sum of Math and EBRW scores
      • Maximum possible score: 1600 üìù

    What are average marks scored by Indian Students

    Rough Idea of Average SAT Scores for Students from India:

    • Approximate Range:
      • Historically, average scores for Indian students have often fallen in the range of 1100-1300 out of 1600.
    • Competitive Nature:
      • Indian students tend to aim for higher scores to remain competitive in the college admissions process.
